I received his M.Sc. degree in Information Technology/Computer Science from the University of Rostock. Then I worked as a research scientist for Siemens AG, Corporate Technology and Deutsche Telekom AG, T-Systems where I participated in many international research projects. In this context, I designed and implemented many new and highly efficient solutions for telecommunications, telematics and industrial applications resulting in several patent applications and publications. I obtained my doctorate with honors from the University of Hamburg. In my doctoral thesis entitled "Model-based Performance Evaluation and Optimization of Multi-Core Architectures for Packet Processing in Communication Networks" (grade: summa cum laude), I developed a new modeling approach for the intra-node resource management in communication networks which I implemented and integrated as an open source module for the network simulator ns-3. Based on that, I proposed and implemented a new ingress QoS concept for Linux-based packet processing (e.g. software routers) to optimize the performance of latency-sensitive applications. My publications received multiple best paper awards on international conferences. I was awarded a one-year fellowship as a visiting postdoc researcher at the University of California, Berkeley in the International Computer Science Institute (ICSI) as well as the Berkeley NetSys Lab. Currently, I am a visiting researcher at the Institute of Communication Networks (ComNets) at Hamburg University of Technology. In addition, I am involved in EIT Digital for the transfer of scientific knowledge to industry to open up new application areas and business models.

My research focuses on communication networks and computer systems which includes the interaction of networked, embedded, and distributed systems. I am a systems researcher and love to build real (software) prototype systems and conduct testbed experiments with them, but I also use modeling and simulation methods for performance evaluations of complex systems. In particular, I am interested in research and development for the Internet of Things (IoT), Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS), Autonomous Systems, Edge & Fog Computing, Software-Defined Networks (SDN), and Network Function Virtualization (NFV).

Selected Projects

Cloud Computing/Edge Computing

  • DAAD project with UC Berkeley, International Computer Science Institute and EIT Digital
  • Specification and implementation of an open carrier interface (OCI) management framework for the orchestration of third-party edge applications for scalabe, reliable and fault-tolerant network applications

Software-Defined Networking/Network Functions Virtualization

  • DFG project MEMPHIS with University of Hamburg and TU Munich
  • Measurement, modeling and simulation of multi-core architectures to identify, predict and optimize performance bottlenecks in software-based packet processing in high-speed networks

Heterogeneous Systems

  • EU project OMEGA with Siemens AG, Infineon AG, Orange Labs, Telefonica, IHP, RWTH Aachen, TU Dortmund, University of Rome, University of Oxford
  • Specification and implementation of the Inter-MAC architecture to connect heterogeneous communication networks (e.g. IEEE 802.11b/g/n, 802.15.4, UWB, 60 GHz, PLC, VLC, IR, Ethernet) taking into account quality of service (QoS) requirements for the L2 path selection (QoS Path Selection, L2-Routing)

Industrial Internet of Things/Wireless Sensor Networks

  • BMBF project ZESAN with Siemens AG, QVEDIS GmbH, ifak Magdeburg, Fraunhofer-Gesellschaft, German-Sino Lab for Mobile Communications, TU Berlin, University of Freiburg
  • Design and implementation of a reliable, energy-efficient and modular communication protocol stack of wireless sensor and actuator networks for building automation, plant monitoring and process control

Wireless Backbone Network

  • University of Rostock research project
  • Implementation, measurement and evaluation of a wireless access and backbone network (multi-channel ad-hoc network) for broadband internet access based on WiMAX/WLAN technologies
